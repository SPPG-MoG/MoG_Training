Overview — What is Ministerial Correspondence?

Understanding your role and the types of cases you will be working with

ℹ️
Welcome

All correspondence for SPPG is issued from the DoH Private Office. Depending on the case type, it will land in your Directorate co-ordination mailbox. Your job is to respond accurately, on time, and in the correct format every time.

📋
AQW / AQO

Assembly Questions

Written questions from MLAs tabled daily to hold Ministers to account. Two types: Ordinary Written (4-day return) and Priority Written (1-2 day return).

✅
COR / SCORR

Correspondence Cases

Letters from MPs, MLAs, Councillors, or CEOs/Chairs of Trusts. The Minister signs off on these. Return within 10 working days.

👤
TOF / STOF

Treat as Official

Letters from members of the public or Councillors. Answered directly on the Minister's behalf. Does NOT return to Private Office unless flagged. Return: 10-14 working days.

📅
INV / SINV

Invitation Cases

Invitations for the Minister/Perm Sec to attend events or meetings. Two-stage process: Advice and Reply, then (if accepted) full Briefing.

📬
GM

General Mail

Letters or circulars that do not fit the above categories. Issued for information only. If a response is needed, notify Private Office to reclassify as COR or TOF.

⚠️
The S Prefix

You will notice SCORR, SINV, STOF - the S stands for Secretarial, meaning the Permanent Secretary is in post rather than a Minister. Handle these identically, but route to the Perm Sec office.

Non-negotiable rule

AQ deadlines are set by the Northern Ireland Assembly Standing Orders - not by Private Office. Missing them is not an option. Extensions are only granted in exceptional circumstances and must be requested via email on or before the day it is due.

Frequently Asked Questions

What do I do if I receive a request not via the official process? ▼
Forward it immediately to your Directorate co-ordination mailbox. It must be processed through the official route via the Minister/Permanent Secretary office. The onus is on whoever receives it to route it correctly.
Can I use a standard response if I have answered a similar question before? ▼
Text from previous similar responses can be used, but you must ensure the specific issues raised have been fully addressed. It is the drafter's responsibility that the response is accurate and complete.
A case has been allocated to me but relates to another Directorate - what do I do? ▼
Contact the co-ordination team. If the main element sits with another Directorate, the co-ordination team will arrange a formal transfer via the Minister/Perm Sec office. You must provide the email of acceptance of the transfer to Private Office. AQs will not be reallocated without prior agreement between branches.
When does a complaint become a TOF? ▼
When an enquirer writes directly to, or copies in, the Permanent Secretary or Minister. At that point it enters the formal correspondence system and must be treated accordingly.
What training is available? ▼
HSC Learning Centre provides: Machinery of Government (core course), HSC eLearning - Working with Minister, and HSC Face-to-Face Training - Working with Minister. Guides and hints are also produced by Private Office and available on the Insight intranet.

Assembly Questions — AQW & AQO

Everything you need to know about written and oral Assembly Questions

📖
Why do AQs matter?

"It is of paramount importance that Ministers give accurate and truthful information to the Assembly, correcting any inadvertent error at the earliest opportunity. Ministers who knowingly mislead the Assembly shall be expected to resign." Your draft is the Minister's answer on public record.

📝
AQW - Ordinary Written

Ordinary Written

Standard written question from an MLA. Each MLA can ask up to 5 questions daily. Deadline to Private Office: 4 working days.

⚡
AQW - Priority Written

Priority Written

Urgent written question - each MLA may include one priority per day. Deadline to Private Office: 1-2 working days.

🎤
AQO - Oral

Oral Questions

Questions put to Ministers in the Assembly chamber. Involves agreement with Directorate, Service Lead and AQ Team before submission to Central Engagement Team.

Format Requirements

// AQW Answer Template - Key Requirements

Line spacing: 1.5 (answer) / Single (question text)
Alignment: Justified
Format: Microsoft Word ONLY - no tables in picture or Excel format
Background note: Arial, size 14, 1.5 spaced - max 1 A4 page, numbered paragraphs
Margins: DO NOT alter from template

💡
Referring to a previous answer

If a similar question was answered within the same Assembly session (within a few months), you may use: "I would refer the Member to the reply I gave the Member for [constituency] on [date]."

🚨
Errors in published answers

If you discover a factual error in a published AQ answer, notify Private Office immediately. Do not wait. Private Office will inform the Minister and the Assembly Business Office once a correction is cleared.

Correspondence, Invitations & Briefings

CORs, TOFs, INVs - how to prepare and submit each type

COR - Correspondence Case

ElementRequirement
Who sends it?MPs, MLAs, Councillors, CEO or Chair of Trust/Board
Reference formate.g. COR-1234-2020 or SCORR-1125-2023
Timeline10 working days. Routine clearance = min 8 working days; Urgent = min 2; Immediate = same day (notify PO in advance)
Who signs off?The Minister (or Perm Sec for SCORRs)
Trust involvementSend to Trust mailbox. If complaint or patient inquiry, refer to Trust to respond directly
ConsentRequired if the case involves patient/personal data. Use the Regional Consent Form or SPPG Third Party Consent Form

COR Advice and Draft Reply - What Your Submission Needs

FROM / TO / DATE

Name, branch, date. Clearance route: 1. Senior Official, then 2. Minister/Perm Sec.

SUMMARY block

Issue (1-2 sentences), Timing (Routine/Urgent/Immediate), Presentational Issues (engage Press Office - never assume None), FOI Implications, Executive Referral, Financial/Legislation/Equality/Rural Needs Implications, SpAd Comments, Recommendation.

Introduction

1-2 sentences: who wrote, when, on what subject.

Background

Numbered paragraphs. Include the original letter at an Annex. Explain the issue and the Department's current position.

Recommendation

"It is recommended that Minister issues the attached response to [Name] MLA providing an update on / advising that... (Attached at Annex A)"

Annex A - Draft Reply Letter

Addressed to the correspondent. Include Your Ref, Our Ref, Date. Professional, proofread, accurate. Signed as Minister of Health MLA / Permanent Secretary.

⚠️
Copied to list (CORs)

Always include: Special Adviser, Private Secretary, Private Office mailbox. For SCORRs: Perm Sec mailbox, Kim Burns, and relevant DoH staff. Press Office should always be in the loop.

TOF - Treat as Official

👤
Key difference

TOFs are NOT returned to Private Office for clearance before issue (unless indicated on Action Sheet). Issued directly to the member of the public via the Office Manager using the No Reply mailbox. Signed off by the relevant senior manager - does NOT require AD approval. A copy must be provided to Private Office by the deadline.

INV - Invitation Cases

📋

Stage 1 - Advice and Draft Reply

Officials advise the Minister on whether to accept or decline. Provide full justification. Draft both acceptance and decline letters if it is a close call.

📁

Stage 2 - Briefing Pack (if accepted)

Only triggered if Minister accepts. Full briefing pack including agenda, pen pictures, speaking notes/speech, Q&A, Lines to Take, Press Release (cleared by Press Office).

💡
Briefing - Addressing the Minister

Always use the second person: "You will be meeting..." rather than "The Minister will be meeting..." Speaking notes use bulleted lines (not verbatim). Speeches are written verbatim, read aloud to check flow, all acronyms written out in full.

Holding Letter Template

// Standard Holding Letter

Dear MLA,

Thank you for your email of [DATE] requesting [BRIEF DESCRIPTION].
I am sorry to hear about [e.g. your constituent's ongoing health problems].

Your request has been forwarded to [X Trust] who will provide
you with a detailed response by [DATE] at the latest.

Yours Sincerely, Minister of Health
⚠️
Holding letters - use sparingly

Not to be issued routinely. They must always be accompanied by a SUB to Private Office explaining the delay.

04

Case Process Flows

Step-by-step journeys for each case type from Private Office to submission

🔵 AQW - Ordinary Written Assembly Question ▼

Private Office issues case via Knowledge Network (KN)

AQ reference allocated e.g. AQW 1234/17-22. Lands in Directorate co-ordination mailbox.

Case received and logged by AQ Team

Allocated to appropriate Service Lead based on subject matter, locality, or specialism.

Directorate Determination

Can the Directorate lead? If no, transfer sought to appropriate Directorate. Await confirmation. Update ownership and reassign Service Lead.

Service Lead contacted, question provided

Service Lead drafts response. Stakeholder input sought if required.

Draft returned to AQ Team

Co-Director clearance obtained. Final version provided back to AQ Team.

Quality Assured

Format, content, accuracy checked against good practice guidelines.

Submitted to Private Office

Email to private.office@health-ni.gov.uk with CM link and Word document attached. Relevant officials copied.

🟠 AQO - Oral Assembly Question ▼

Forthcoming questions issued by Private Office

Earlier than AQW - AQOs need more lead time for preparation.

Agreement reached

Between Directorate, Service Lead and AQ Team on handling and ownership.

Confirmation to Private Office within timeframe

Service Lead drafts response

Stakeholder input sought if required.

Co-Director approval then Director approval

Both levels of clearance required for AQOs.

Submitted to Central Engagement Team

Quality assured before onward submission to Private Office.

🟢 COR - Correspondence Case ▼

Correspondence received by Private Office

Scanned letter attached to Action Sheet, deadline indicated.

Directorate Determination

Can Directorate lead? If no, transfer to appropriate Directorate with formal acceptance email to PO.

Service Lead contacted, case details provided

Check: Is consent required? If yes, consent sought via AQ Team before proceeding.

Service Lead drafts response

Stakeholder input if required. Check: Is a holding letter or extension needed?

Extension or Holding Letter decision

If yes, request raised to Private Office, await approval. If no, proceed directly.

Co-Director approval, Quality Assured, Submitted to Private Office

Minister/Perm Sec signs and issues.

🔴 TOF - Treat as Official ▼

TOF received

From a member of the public, service user, carer, or Councillor.

Consent check

If the case involves patient data, consent must be sought before Trust is contacted.

Service Lead drafts response

Trust input if required. Uses TOF template from SharePoint.

Signed off by relevant Senior Manager

Does NOT require AD approval. No return to Private Office for clearance unless Action Sheet indicates otherwise.

Issued directly to member of public

Via Office Manager using No Reply mailbox. Copy provided to Private Office by deadline.

🟣 INV - Invitation Case (Two-Stage) ▼

Stage 1 - Advice and Draft Reply

INV received, draft advice and reply prepared

Officials advise Minister on accept/decline with full justification.

Consent checked if applicable

Service Lead drafts response and seeks stakeholder input

Co-Director approval, Quality Assured, Submitted to Private Office

Stage 2 - Briefing (only if Minister ACCEPTS)

INV Briefing received

Cover note, background, plus requested elements: Speaking Notes, Speech, Q&A, Lines to Take, Pen Pictures, Agenda, Press Release.

Service Lead drafts briefing, stakeholder input if required

Press Office must clear any speeches/speaking notes before inclusion

Being copied on a briefing is NOT the same as approving. Factor in Press Office clearance time.

Co-Director approval, Quality Assured, Submitted to Private Office
